[ConcHIP] Flow-Aware Resource Predicates for Concurrency Verification

By Duy-Khanh Le, Wei-Ngan Chin, Yong Meng Teo and Shengchao Qin

ConcHIP's Overview

ConcHIP is a verifier for shared-memory concurrent programs. It employs "flow-aware resource predicate" for precisely keeping track of resources flows. Resources of such a predicate can be more flexibly split and transferred across procedure and thread boundaries. While originally motivated by the need to support first-class threads, the proposal for flow-aware resource predicate is general, i.e. it can handle other popular concurrency mechanisms including CountDownLatch and locks.
